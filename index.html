GMV is up.
So why is profit flat?

Today's ranking sees Ads cost but not Affiliate commission, even though both come out of the seller's pocket. Affiliate Cost Optimization closes that blind spot — the system now optimizes for profit, not just GMV.

01 / THE BLIND SPOT

Without ACO, the system sees one cost.
The seller still pays two.

Ads cost is in the ranking equation. Affiliate commission is not. For shops with high commission rates, that is a real chunk of money the system never weighs.

Both creatives drive the same GMV. A pays $17 more in commission than B. The system only looks at Ads cost, so the two creatives look identical to it.

That is the gap ACO closes.

05 / WHAT CHANGES FOR THE SELLER

Nothing for you to change.
The system just gets smarter underneath.

Same campaign. Same Target ROI input. Same creators and contracts. The system just picks differently behind the scenes.

Stays the same

  • ✓Existing Product GMV Max campaign
  • ✓Same Target ROI input in Ads Manager
  • ✓Same products, same budget, same creative workflow
  • ✓Affiliate program and commission contracts untouched

What ACO does

  • ✓Ranks creatives on what's left after both Ads cost and Affiliate commission — not just Ads cost.
  • ✓When two creatives drive similar GMV, the cheaper one wins traffic.
  • ✓High-commission creatives still win when they clearly drive more GMV.
  • ✓You keep the same GMV (or more) while paying less in total cost.
